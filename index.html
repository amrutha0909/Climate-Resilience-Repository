Steps To Resilience



Climate resilience is a new concept to many. It is all about preparing ourselves to face challenges related to climate change and coming up with solutions for sustainable development.
Here are some steps to climate resilience:


1.Understanding Exposure

Identify the things your community cares about List the climate-related hazards you could face.

Examples: Flooding, drought, hurricane, heat wave, landsliding, lightning, etc.

Answer these questions:

  1. What hazards have occurred in your region in the past?
  2. What hazards might occur over the next decade or so?
  3. What hazards might you face over the next several decades?
  4. Could rising sea levels impact your region?
  5. Which of your assets are exposed to weather and climate?

Imagine and describe potential consequences

Recognize the role of stressors

2.Assess Vulnerabilities and Risk

The first element of risk is the probability of a hazard occurring.

  • How likely is it that the hazard will happen in your location?
  • How frequently has it occurred in the past, and is that frequency increasing due to climate change?

The second element is the magnitude of consequences from the event.

  • Would the hazard cause a major disruption for a large number of people for an extended period?
  • Would it require large amounts of money and time to regain the previous level of function?

3.Investigate Options

Brainstorm potential solutions

  • Develop a wide-ranging list of strategies that could reduce risk.

Learn from others

  • Explore case studies and adaptation planning resources.

Evaluate potential solutions

  • Decide which of your options represent viable actions.

Refine your goals

  • Consider what you’ve learned and reassess where you’re going.

Decision point

  • Are community members committed to the goals you identified?

4.Prioritise and Plan

Consolidate actions into a cohesive plan

  • Combine similar actions and sequence them to reduce risk across assets.

Estimate the expected value of each action

  • Assess whether investments will reduce risk.

Evaluate trade-offs

  • In light of limited resources, decide what you can do.

Plan the project

  • Develop a timeline and milestones to mark your progress.

5.Final Action

Implement your plan

Monitor your results

Iterate as needed

  • Watch for opportunities to improve or recover from missteps.
