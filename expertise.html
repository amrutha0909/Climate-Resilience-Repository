Reports

Here are some recent climate change reports published in India along with links to access them:



1. India State of Forest Report (ISFR) 2023: This report provides comprehensive information on forest cover, biodiversity, and carbon sequestration in India. It is published by the Forest Survey of India (FSI).
Link: ISFR 2023
2. Climate Change Performance Index (CCPI) 2024: The CCPI ranks countries based on their performance in combating climate change. India's ranking and performance are included in this global report.
Link: CCPI 2022
3. India Cooling Action Plan (ICAP) 2024: This plan outlines strategies to mitigate the impact of increasing cooling demand on energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions in India.
Link: ICAP 2024
4. India's National Communication to the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC): This communication includes India's climate change mitigation and adaptation efforts, policies, and actions.
Link: India's National Communication to UNFCCC
5. Annual Report on Implementation of Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs): This report assesses India's progress in meeting its climate commitments outlined in its NDCs under the Paris Agreement.
Link: NDC Implementation Report
6. State Action Plans on Climate Change (SAPCCs): Various states in India have developed SAPCCs outlining their strategies and actions to address climate change at the state level. These reports are available on respective state government websites.
Example: SAPCC
 7. Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) Special Report on Climate Change and Land: While not specific to India, this report provides insights into global land-use practices, deforestation, and their impact on climate change, which are relevant to India's context.
Link: IPCC Special Report on Climate Change and Land

These reports offer valuable information on India's climate change policies, actions, and the state of environmental sustainability. Please note that some reports may require registration or access through official government portals.

Tools

Here are some climate resilience planning tools in India along with links to access them:



1. National Adaptation Fund for Climate Change (NAFCC) Online Platform: • Description: The NAFCC platform provides information and resources for climate change adaptation planning, funding opportunities, and project implementation.
• Link:NAFCC Online Platform
2. Climate Resilience Index (CRI):
• Description: The CRI is a tool developed by the Indian Institute of Public Administration (IIPA) to assess the climate resilience of Indian states and districts.
• Link: Climate Resilience Index
3. Climate Resilience Knowledge Portal:
• Description: This portal, developed by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC), provides information, data, and tools for climate resilience planning at various levels.
• Link: Climate Resilience Knowledge Portal
4. Climate Adaptation Planning Toolkit (CAPT):
• Description: CAPT is a toolkit developed by the Centre for Development Finance (CDF), National Institute of Public Finance and Policy (NIPFP), to support local governments in climate adaptation planning.
• Link: Climate Adaptation Planning Toolkit
5. Adaptation Policy and Practice Database:
• Description: The database, managed by the Climate Change Knowledge Portal, provides information on adaptation policies, practices, and tools in India and globally.
• Link: Adaptation Policy and Practice Database
6. Climate Smart Agriculture (CSA) Planning Tool:
• Description: The CSA planning tool, developed by the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR), assists farmers and stakeholders in implementing climate-resilient agriculture practices.
• Link: CSA Planning Tool
7. Community-Based Disaster Risk Reduction (CBDRR) Toolkit:
• Description: The CBDRR toolkit, developed by the National Institute of Disaster Management (NIDM), supports communities in building resilience to climate-related disasters.
• Link: CBDRR Toolkit

These tools and platforms offer valuable resources and guidance for climate resilience planning, adaptation, and risk reduction efforts in India. Users can access them to enhance their understanding of climate impacts and develop strategies for building resilience at various levels.

Here are some climate science and service centers in India:

1.Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology (IITM), Pune:
Conducts research on tropical meteorology, climate variability, and climate change.



2. National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting (NCMRWF), Noida:
Focuses on medium-range weather forecasting and climate modeling.



3. National Centre for Antarctic and Ocean Research (NCAOR), Goa:
Conducts research on polar and oceanographic sciences, including climate studies related to Antarctica.



4. Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services (INCOIS), Hyderabad:
Provides oceanographic information and services, including climate-related data and forecasts.



5. Indian Meteorological Department (IMD), New Delhi:
Government agency responsible for weather forecasting, monitoring climate patterns, and issuing weather advisories.



6. Centre for Climate Change Research (CCCR), Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology, Pune:
Conducts research on climate change impacts and adaptation strategies.



7. National Institute of Oceanography (NIO), Goa:
Conducts research on oceanography, marine ecology, and climate-related studies.



8. National Institute of Hydrology (NIH), Roorkee:
Focuses on hydrological research, including climate change impacts on water resources.



9. Centre for Atmospheric Sciences (CAS), Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi:
Conducts research on atmospheric processes, climate modeling, and air quality.



10. Wadia Institute of Himalayan Geology (WIHG), Dehradun:
Conducts research on Himalayan geology, glaciology, and climate change impacts in the Himalayan region.



11. Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Bengaluru:
Uses satellite data for climate monitoring, weather forecasting, and environmental studies.



12. National Institute of Disaster Management (NIDM), New Delhi:
Focuses on disaster risk reduction, including climate-related disasters and adaptation strategies.



13. Climate Resilience Centre, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Mandi:
Conducts research on climate resilience, adaptation strategies, and sustainable development.



14. Climate Change and Sustainable Development Division, The Energy and Resources Institute (TERI), New Delhi:
Conducts research and provides policy recommendations on climate change mitigation and adaptation.



15. National Centre for Earth Science Studies (NCESS), Thiruvananthapuram:
